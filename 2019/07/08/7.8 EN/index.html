July 8th

First day in the third week. First half day that is rainy, first rainy morning, the seventh rain in Baltimore when I arrived.

We have our second quiz today. Unfortunately, I still don’t get a perfect score. Although it shows a good percentage (204/220, 93%), will, actually is not quite good. I have been a bad student for so long time that I have forget whether it is normal and mandatory for me to get a score higher than 95% or it is super good for me. I hope it is not my liit!

After the quiz we start to build our paper mousetrap. Unfortunately, mine is not working because of too much friction. Definitely need to get a new model before Friday deadline!

We have our first finance class this afternoon, which is also the second big class we have after the electronics lecture. The finance lecture this afternoon actually surprised me because it has a big difference from what I expected to. The deepest impression that EI gave me is that many of courses that previously I thought as a part that the program holders just want us to have some “classes” in this program, like a part to fill out the class schedule. For finance, I have expected to teach us some economic ideas like price mechanisms, Inflation rates or something related to business. However, in the finance class this afternoon, the professor introduced a variety of algorithms to make a profit. All these beautiful and complicated logarithms like the logarithm to get e (natural base number) let me understand why economics and finance courses can be classified as STEM classes. The essence of whether finance, economics or business is a general rule or result gotten as a conclusion that is calculated using a series of mathematical formulas, such as price elasticity of demand/supply. Logic essence of price mechanism and government policies makes all these financial and economical knowledges convincing and looks reasonable.

The special lecture held this afternoon is spoken by a counselor, Miss Bianca Decatur, who is responsible for California’s applicant. Yes, this school is sexy and gorgeous, especially for those biomedical applicants. The university has a high teacher-student ratio (teacher: student 1:7) and a relatively small class range like we have in EI (24 people in one class). This is a school that really supports student research. With only 10% of international students in all of the university undergraduates, 72% of the undergraduates get internships when studying in JHU. Someone even gets a paid internship. You can even apply for up to $10,000 in off-campus research funding. The life for undergraduate students in such a rich school is just like a dream. However, the upper and lower quartiles of the SAT that are extremely high (1480-1550). It is not unreasonable to be a partner of Ivy League School for Johns Hopkins University. Unfortunately, Hopkins’s strongest professional, biomedical engineering, is not my point of interest. What a pity. Otherwise, this can really be said to be a dream school for me.

I have swum 1600m tonight, which is much longer than I swam previous days. I swam 1500m of 1600m continuously and it takes me about 35’33’’73’’’. Actually, I finished 1500m within 32’30’’ because I need to change my direction (arriving the 2 sides of swimming pool). Each change takes about 3s. I need to hurry up in order to finish 1500m within 30min!

Hurry up tomorrow!
